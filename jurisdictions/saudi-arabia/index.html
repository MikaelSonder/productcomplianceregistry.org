Core Function
Product compliance, product safety, technical regulation and conformity responsibility for products placed on the Saudi Arabian market.
Primary Interfaces
Product Safety Law, Standards and Quality Law, SASO technical regulations, SABER certificates of conformity, product and shipment certificates, market surveillance and economic operator obligations.
Cross-Border Note
Saudi Arabia uses a structured conformity assessment system through SABER to ensure imported and domestic products meet technical regulations, while the Product Safety Law applies to products traded through conventional and electronic means except those under the Saudi Food and Drug Authority.
Executive Summary

Product compliance in Saudi Arabia is the structured function through which products are reviewed against the Product Safety Law, technical regulations, SASO standards, conformity expectations and market surveillance exposure before and during placement on the Saudi market.

In practice, the subject is wider than certification alone because businesses must first determine which Saudi technical rules apply, whether the product is regulated under specific technical regulations and how conformity certificates, SABER registration and documentation must be arranged before import, distribution or sale.

The Saudi system is shaped by a new Product Safety Law, a Standards and Quality Law, technical regulations issued by SASO, conformity assessment bodies, SABER product and shipment certificates and market surveillance activities aimed at removing unsafe products from the market and preventing discrimination between domestic and imported goods.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because most regulated products shipped to Saudi Arabia need appropriate certificates of conformity and SABER registration before customs clearance and market entry can occur.

Process Flow

The process flow explains how product compliance work usually progresses from product identification to conformity execution and later maintenance. It matters because compliance is a practical sequence, not a single approval event.

1. Product IdentificationIdentify the product, its intended use, risk profile, technical characteristics and proposed route to market in Saudi Arabia.
2. Regulation MappingDetermine which Saudi technical regulations and SASO standards apply to the product category.
3. Conformity Assessment PlanningSelect an appropriate conformity route, assign a SASO-approved certification body if required and plan testing and documentation.
4. SABER Product RegistrationRegister the product in SABER, identify relevant technical regulations and upload required documents and test reports.
5. Product Certificate of ConformityObtain a Product Certificate of Conformity once documentation and testing are approved; typically valid for one year.
6. Shipment Certificate of ConformityFor each shipment, apply for a Shipment Certificate of Conformity linked to the product certificate before customs clearance.
7. Customs Clearance and Market PlacementUse valid conformity certificates to pass customs and place products on the Saudi market.
8. Monitoring and ResponseMonitor safety performance, complaints, inspections and regulatory updates, and respond to market surveillance activities or corrective requirements.
Typical OutputsRegulation mapping file, test reports, technical documentation, product certificate, shipment certificates and labeling evidence.
Decision Tree

The decision tree simplifies threshold questions that commonly determine the correct product compliance route in Saudi Arabia.

  1. Identify the product and its intended use in Saudi Arabia.
  2. Determine which technical regulations and SASO standards apply.
  3. Confirm whether the product is regulated under specific technical regulations or covered by general product safety obligations.
  4. Select a conformity assessment body and plan testing and documentation where required.
  5. Register the product in SABER and obtain a Product Certificate of Conformity.
  6. Obtain Shipment Certificates of Conformity for each shipment before customs clearance.
  7. Maintain documentation, monitor safety performance and respond to surveillance activities.

FAQ

The FAQ section collects recurring threshold questions in a concise handbook format.

Do Products Placed on the Saudi Market Need to Meet Product Safety Law Requirements?Yes. Products must comply with the Product Safety Law and relevant technical regulations, and unsafe products may not be manufactured, imported, marketed, distributed or advertised.
What Is SASO’s Role?SASO develops and implements standards, technical regulations and conformity assessment procedures to ensure product safety and quality.
What Is a SASO Certificate of Conformity?It is a mandatory document for regulated products confirming that goods meet Saudi technical regulations, safety requirements and quality standards and is linked to customs clearance.
What Is SABER?SABER is an electronic platform used to manage product registration, product certificates of conformity and shipment certificates for goods entering Saudi Arabia.
Can a Foreign Company Need Saudi-Specific Compliance Planning?Yes. Foreign companies exporting to Saudi Arabia often need Saudi-specific regulation mapping, SABER account coordination, testing and conformity planning.
